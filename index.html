Howdy! Welcome to my website. I am a sophomore computer science major at Texas A&M University. Engineering has always been an integral part of my life, and it is important to me that I continue to hone my skills by challenging myself both within and beyond the classroom. I hope to share my efforts with you here.

In addition to providing general information about me, this website serves as a repository of my personal accomplishments and endeavors. Feel free to explore my pages using the navigation bar above. My goal is for you to have a good idea of what I am capable of by the end of your visit.

A Little More About Me

Background

I was born and raised in The Woodlands, Texas. My father spent most of his life in Texas, too, but my mother hails from Thailand. This, of course, makes me half Thai! My family and I visit Thailand often to spend time with relatives.

I attended the Academy of Science and Technology at The Woodlands College Park High School. I competed in science fairs throughout my time there, earning several awards along the way. The process of developing research projects and presenting them to teachers, peers, and industry professionals was grueling at times, but I consider science fair to be one of my most valuable academic experiences.

I was always intrigued by technology, but taking computer science electives in high school and incorporating that knowledge into some of my science fair projects are what solidified my choice to pursue further study in the field. Now, as a college student, I am excited to build my skills and apply them in the real world.

Hobbies

I started playing the trombone in intermediate school, and I have yet to put my horn down. I was a member of my high school's wind ensemble and a section leader in our marching band. Today, I play the trombone for the University Symphonic Band at Texas A&M.

Beyond that, I enjoy editing videos (usually skits starring me and/or my friends) and finishing 3D printed models. I recently finished painting a clone trooper helmet from Star Wars that I am quite proud of.
